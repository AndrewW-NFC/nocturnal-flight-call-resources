NFC acoustic environment forecast

Score hourly weather data for sound clarity and propagation—the acoustic environment for nocturnal flight call recording.

It does not forecast bird activity or migration. Such forecasts are available from BirdCast.

Use auto or an IANA value such as America/New_York.

Click the map or drag the marker to select latitude and longitude. Requires an internet connection.

Leafed-out trees can add rustle and intermittent broadband noise in wind, raising the noise floor and masking faint calls or fine spectrogram detail.

Some insect sound bands and pulses can mask NFC structure on the spectrogram.

Not weighted in score. Estimates whether nearby major roads and runway corridors may carry traffic or aircraft noise in the direction of the recorder. May add 30+ seconds to forecast processing.

Notes

      
    
        
  • Weather data: 10 m wind is from Open-Meteo. Near-ground wind is estimated from the same profile. Visibility uses API data when available; otherwise, it is estimated from precipitation and cloud cover.
    • 
        
  • Night windows: Night windows use astronomical twilight from sunrise-sunset.org.
    • 
        
  • Scoring: The weighted score emphasizes factors that can mask bird calls or reduce visible structure on the spectrogram, including wind noise, precipitation, humidity, visibility, atmospheric stability, foliage noise, and insect noise.
    • 
        
  • Foliage: Foliage noise is set by the user. Use a higher setting when leafed-out trees are close to the recorder.
    • 
        
  • Insects: Insect noise is included in the weighted score when selected because some insect bands and pulses can overlap nocturnal flight-call frequencies and mask NFC structure on the spectrogram.
    • 
        
  • Human-made noise: Road and aircraft context is shown separately from the weighted score because actual source strength varies by traffic volume, flight activity, pavement conditions, and other factors the tool cannot directly measure.
    • 
        
  • Road traffic: Road traffic is estimated from nearby OpenStreetMap roads tagged motorway, trunk, or primary; distance to the selected point; road class; wind direction; and that night’s calm, humid, stable conditions.
    • 
        
  • Aircraft: Aircraft context is estimated from nearby mapped or built-in major airports and mapped runway alignment. It does not use live or scheduled flight data.
    • 
        
  • “Could not check”: This means the lookup service failed, timed out, or returned incomplete data. It does not mean the site is quiet.
